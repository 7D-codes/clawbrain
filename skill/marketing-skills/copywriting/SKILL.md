## Copywriting Principles

### 1. Lead with Outcome, Not Feature
‚ùå "ClawBrain uses OpenClaw for AI integration"
‚úÖ "Your AI assistant that actually knows your work"

### 2. Use Customer Language
Mirror how users describe their problems:
- "I have notes in 5 different apps"
- "ChatGPT doesn't remember what I told it"
- "I want AI that knows my projects"

### 3. Be Specific, Not Vague
‚ùå "Boost your productivity"
‚úÖ "Create tasks by chatting with your AI"

### 4. Address Objections Head-On
- "Self-hosted" ‚Üí addresses privacy concerns
- "Just markdown files" ‚Üí addresses lock-in fears
- "Open source" ‚Üí addresses trust issues

### 5. One Idea Per Section
Don't cram everything. Let each section breathe.

## Homepage Copy Structure

### Hero Section

**Formula:** [Problem] + [Solution] + [Outcome]

**Example:**
```
Headline: Your AI Second Brain
Subhead: Chat with your tasks. Own your data. Finally, an AI that knows your work.
CTA: Get Early Access
```

**Alternative angles:**
- Privacy-first: "The AI assistant that runs on your machine"
- AI-native: "Chat-first productivity for the AI era"
- Open source: "Your second brain, open source and self-hosted"

### Problem Section

**Formula:** Agitate the pain, then hint at solution

**Example:**
```
Your notes are scattered. Your AI doesn't remember. Your data is in the cloud.

You have a tool for tasks. A tool for notes. A tool for AI. None of them talk to each other.

What if one tool did it all ‚Äî and kept your data where it belongs?
```

### Solution Section

**Formula:** Feature + Benefit + Proof

**Example:**
```
Chat creates tasks. Kanban organizes them. AI understands everything.

Just type "Remind me to follow up with Sarah tomorrow" ‚Äî it appears in your board, context and all.

All stored as simple markdown files. Your data, your control.
```

1. Chat naturally ‚Äî "What tasks do I have this week?"

## Landing Page Copy Checklist

For any ClawBrain landing page, ensure:

- [ ] Headline focuses on outcome (not feature)
- [ ] Subhead explains what it is in one sentence
- [ ] CTA is clear and action-oriented
- [ ] Problem section agitates real pain
- [ ] Solution shows the "magic moment" (chat ‚Üí task)
- [ ] Features translated to benefits
- [ ] Social proof (testimonials, logos, stats)
- [ ] FAQ addresses objections
- [ ] Final CTA repeats main action

---

## Common Copy Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Don't say "AI-powered"** ‚Äî Everyone says this. Be specific: "Chat creates your tasks"

2. **Don't lead with "OpenClaw"** ‚Äî Most people don't know what this is. Lead with the benefit.

3. **Don't use "solution"** ‚Äî It's vague corporate speak. Say what you actually do.

4. **Don't over-explain the tech** ‚Äî "Bidirectional sync" is implementation. "Chat and board stay in sync" is the benefit.

5. **Don't forget the CTA** ‚Äî Every section should drive toward an action.
